غلطهای رایج زبان انگلیسی


جملات زیر بیست غلط رایجی بوده که زبانان اغلب در گفتار و یا نوشتارخود به کار می برند.




Discuss about something
Correct: discuss something
We discussed many subjects with each other.

Succeed to doing something
Correct: succeed in doing something
I finally succeeded in getting the admission.

Respect to someone/something
Correct: respect somebody/something
Some of children do not respect the old people.

Get married with someone
Correct: get married to someone
She got married to him two years ago.

Spend something for someone/something
Correct: spend something on someone/something
He spent all his money on shopping.

Between ... to
Correct: between ... and
Unemployment rate was on decline between 2015 and 2019.

Prevent someone to do something
Correct: prevent someone from doing
This rule prevented them from leaving the country.

Stuffs / equipments / advices
Correct: stuff / equipment / advice
We bought all stuff/equipment we needed. / You may need to listen to all her advice.

Congratulate someone for (doing) something
Correct: congratulate someone on (doing) something
First of all, I'd like to congratulate you on your achievement.

Be busy with doing something
Correct: busy doing something
Lately, I have been busy doing many tasks.

Attend to a meeting / class / seminar / …
Correct: attend a meeting / class / seminar
You need to attend your class regularly.

Reach to something/someone
Correct: reach someone/something
When you reach that café, we will meet you.

Go to/live in abroad
Correct: go/live abroad
I would like to live abroad.

Make friend with someone
Correct: make friends with
He easily makes friends with different people from all walks of life.

Sometime
Correct: sometimes/at times
I sometimes make this mistake.

Access to something
Correct: access something
Everywhere you go, you can access the Internet.

Rude / polite / kind with someone
Correct: rude/polite /kind to someone
She is extremely kind to me.

Such something
Correct: such a/an something
It was such a wonderful evening.

Go to/for shopping
Correct: go shopping
We go shopping once a week.

Go to trip
Correct: go on trip
I will go on a business trip next month.
